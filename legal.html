In Short: We only process your personal information when we believe it is necessary and we have a valid legal reason (i.e., legal basis) to do so under applicable law, like with your consent, to comply with laws, to provide you with services to enter into or fulfill our contractual obligations, to protect your rights, or to fulfill our legitimate business interests.

Legal Obligations. If you are located in Canada, this section applies to you.

In some exceptional cases, we may be legally permitted under applicable law to process your information without your consent, including, for example:


If collection is clearly in the interests of an individual and consent cannot be obtained in a timely way

For investigations and fraud detection and prevention

If the collection is solely for journalistic, artistic, or literary purposes

8. CONTROLS FOR DO-NOT-TRACK FEATURES

Most web browsers and some mobile operating systems and mobile applications include a Do-Not-Track ("DNT ") feature or setting you can activate to signal your privacy preference not to have data about your online browsing activities monitored and collected. At this stage no uniform technology standard for recognizing and implementing DNT signals has been finalized. As such, we do not currently respond to DNT browser signals or any other mechanism that automatically communicates your choice not to be tracked online. If a standard for online tracking is adopted that we must follow in the future, we will inform you about that practice in a revised version of this privacy notice.

CCPA Privacy Notice

The California Code of Regulations defines a "resident "as:


(1) every individual who is in the State of California for other than a temporary or transitory purpose and

(2) every individual who is domiciled in the State of California who is outside the State of California for a temporary or transitory purpose

All other individuals are defined as "non-residents."


If this definition of "resident "applies to you, we must adhere to certain rights and obligations regarding your personal information.

10. DO VIRGINIA RESIDENTS HAVE SPECIFIC PRIVACY RIGHTS?

In Short: Yes, if you are a resident of Virginia, you are granted specific rights regarding access to your personal information.


Virginia residents are entitled to specific privacy rights concerning their personal information under the Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act (CDPA). This act defines key terms, such as a "consumer "being a natural person residing in Virginia, acting in an individual or household context, not in a commercial or employment context. "Personal data "refers to information linked or reasonably linkable to an identified or identifiable natural person, excluding de-identified data or publicly available information. The "sale of personal data "entails the exchange of personal data for monetary consideration.

As a consumer, you have certain rights and obligations regarding your personal data. The data collected, used, and disclosed about you depends on your interactions with the company and its services. These rights include the right to be informed about the processing of your personal data, access to your personal data, correction of inaccuracies, deletion of your personal data, obtaining a copy of previously shared data, and opting out of processing for targeted advertising, sale, or profiling. Importantly, the company states it has not sold personal data to third parties for business or commercial purposes and will not do so in the future.

To inquire about these rights or exercise them, you can contact the company via email at social@davidanthonychenault.com or submit a data subject access request. Be aware that a verification process may be required for consumer requests, and the company aims to respond within 45 days, with a one-time extension of 45 additional days if needed. If your request is declined, you have the right to appeal within 60 days.
